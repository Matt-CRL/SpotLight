A Travel Guide to Japan for First-Timers

By: Matt Christian Lara

Wondering how to make the most out of your visit in Japan?

Whether you're planning your first international trip or just curious about what Japan has to offer, this guide is written for you.

Where to Go?

  • Tokyo, Japan’s capital and largest city, features modern attractions, endless shopping options, and vibrant nightlife. It’s truly a megacity that never sleeps, offering countless things to experience. From towering skyscrapers to quiet gardens, Tokyo has something for every kind of traveler.
  • Kyoto is where you'll find the heart of Japan’s history. It’s home to ancient shrines, serene bamboo forests, and traditional tea houses that preserve the country’s rich cultural heritage. If you want to experience Japan’s past, Kyoto is the perfect place to start.
  • Osaka is known for its incredible food. Enjoy strolling through the city while savoring some of Japan’s most delicious cuisine. Beyond its culinary scene, Osaka also offers a variety of attractions, from historic shrines and temples to popular theme parks like Universal Studios Japan (USJ).
Must-Try Foods in Japan
  • Ramen, which comes in many styles depending on the region, is a must-try dish in Japan. Ichiran, a ramen restaurant chain, is famous among foreigners, but locals often say that ramen from small, family-run shops or regional specialties like Hakata-style tonkotsu in Fukuoka or miso ramen in Sapporo offer a more authentic and flavorful experience.
  • Street foods, from grilled fish such as unagi (eel) to savory meats and fresh seafood, are a big part of Japan’s food culture. You can find them in busy markets and festival stalls, offering quick, flavorful bites like takoyaki, yakitori, and taiyaki that are perfect for eating on the go.
  • Konbini foods (Convenience store meals), which are prepared fresh daily. Feeling hungry in the morning or even in the middle of the night, Konbinis offer a variety of munchies for your cravings ranging from onigiris to bentos.
  • Vending Machines, Looking for a quick refresher? Just look around the corner and you’ll spot a vending machine offering unique beverage flavors. From hot coffee in a can to melon soda and even corn soup, they’re a fun and convenient way to stay energized while exploring Japan.
Travel Tips!

Visitors are often surprised by how organized and clean everything is. Trains run on time, and stations are kept spotless by staff. Thus, it is important to help maintain that standard, always remember to clean up after yourself and be mindful of the time, especially at train stations, since some trains don't operate 24/7.

  • Prepare your phone, wifi, and even a powerbank. Being lost while traveling could offer a great experience but having access to maps and directions can help you make the most of your journey.
  • Bring cash with you, as not every store accepts cards. Smaller shops and restaurants often prefer cash payments.
  • Learning the language or even basic phrases might not always be the most effective way to communicate. It’s best to prepare a translator app, as most Japanese people don’t speak English.
  • Keep a small bag for your trash, as public bins are not commonly found in Japan. Carrying your waste until you find a proper disposal spot is a common practice.
