Reality through my eyes

By: Jeirold Kurt Ruiz

Do we see reality as it is?

Reality isn’t reality, is what I initially thought after watching the video. How can I know that there is truly a reality if what I see is just an interpretation of what my eyes can see based on the knowledge I have? When there is far more knowledge I have yet to grasp. Hoffman says that the reality we see and acknowledge is a reality that we construct. “When we see a cube on a screen, it really isn’t a cube but a 2d image, but we perceive it as a cube”, how we look at things is how we construct our reality. Our perception is our window to reality, that is why throughout the years, we’ve had so many interpretations of what our reality is. Before we found out the world was a sphere, we thought it was flat because it looked that way. Different people see things differently, creating different realities.

The physical objects that we see, that we perceive as reality, aren't reality at all. Just a symbol of the “real” world. In my future profession, I would like you to start by broadening my perception on what I truly see, and understand the deeper meanings behind the symbols I come across. So that I can understand the different people I encounter and just how differently they perceive their own realities.

Does the universe really exist when we’re not looking?

As he ponders over his time left in this world, he tries to grasp the question “How come existence?”. It was said that in Quantum Physics, our universe is heavily influenced by our observations. Why? Because the discovery of our universe itself is an observation by people before us. When we look at it, our universe is a giant interaction of various objects, down from the atoms that we can only see through a microscope. Based on what I have read and what really stood out to me was that, what we discover shapes our universe and reality itself. Therefore, is the answer to the title, “Does the universe really exist when we’re not looking?”, no it wouldn’t? Technically speaking, if philosophers didn’t think that there is more to the world than we know; We wouldn’t have come up with the concept of the universe nor would we know that it even exists. No one would truly know what the universe is, what it's made out of, what lies in it, if we weren’t looking. We only discover, when we learn how to ask questions. The universe wouldn’t exist, if we didn’t wonder about it.

Note:

All the statements above are derived from what I have come up with after watching the video linked above. If your thoughts and opinions do not coincide with what I have written. Please know that I do not mean any harm nor offense from my insights.

  • All thoughts and opinion are valid. We all have our own minds that can form unique perspectives and that is acceptable.
  • No opinion is wrong. We are all raised differently and our experiences shape our beliefs.
  • Keep on wondering. Even the littlest thought about how something works can open our minds up on how to see it in a different light.
  • You never know what the universe has in store for you.
